# The AI Eyewear Score — Methodology (v1.0)

An independent 0–100 rating for AI glasses, built from six weighted criteria, measured first-hand, with a disclosed protocol. **The methodology is published before any product is scored.**

> **Status (2026-07-19): 0 devices scored.** This document defines *how* we will rate. A score is assigned only after we have tested a device ourselves under the protocol below. We do not adopt manufacturer scores and we do not invent values.

Canonical version: <https://ai-eyewear.ch/ai-eyewear-score/>

## The six criteria and weights

Base weighting (display / AI glasses such as the Even G2):

| Criterion | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Display & Optics | 22% | Real-world legibility: outdoor brightness/contrast, sharpness, field of view, prescription option |
| Comfort & Design | 20% | Weight & distribution, pressure over hours, fit, build, all-day wearability |
| AI, Software & Updates | 18% | Assistant quality (incl. German/dialect), app & pairing reliability, latency, offline ability, update history |
| Battery & Endurance | 15% | Real mixed-use runtime, charging case, charge time, behaviour under load |
| Privacy & Transparency | 15% | Camera indicator (if any), on-device vs cloud, clear settings & deletion, signalling to bystanders |
| Value | 10% | Price incl. required accessories, running costs, longevity/repairability |

For **camera/audio glasses without a display**, the 22% for "Display & Optics" transfers transparently to **Audio & Camera quality**, so every category is judged fairly rather than on a feature it does not have.

## Test protocol

1. **Disclose sourcing** — bought, borrowed, or loaned; loan units are returned and do not change the score.
2. **Verify specs independently** — weight (scale), brightness/legibility (defined ambient light), battery (measured drain) rather than adopting the spec sheet.
3. **At least two weeks of daily use** — office, on the move, outdoors in daylight.
4. **Standardised, repeatable measurements** — comparable across devices.
5. **Separated scoring** — measurement vs cited third-party test vs editorial judgement; each score names its methodology version and test date.
6. **Corrections & re-scoring** — major software updates can trigger a re-score; errors are corrected openly.

## Independence & funding

- No paid scores; a manufacturer cannot buy a test or a better rating.
- No pre-publication preview; manufacturers cannot change a score.
- The editorial team is separated from commercial operations. AI-Eyewear sells the Even G2 in Switzerland — this is disclosed and gives the G2 no bonus; it is measured on the same protocol as every other device.
- Sourcing, affiliate links, and conflicts of interest are disclosed per test.

## Limits

The Score is an informed but **subjective editorial judgement** on defined measurement points — not a laboratory certificate. Individual units vary; software changes; weightings are a deliberate value choice. That is why they are disclosed: so readers can re-weight for themselves.
